Capstone · Plant Science / CEA

Hemp Hurd & Fish Biosolids as Sustainable Inputs for CEA Tomato Production

Cornell University · M.P.S. Controlled Environment Agriculture · 2026 · Advisor: Dr. Neil Mattson

Two parallel randomized-complete-block greenhouse trials on Edox F1 indeterminate cherry tomato tested whether two agricultural waste streams — hemp hurd (a perlite substrate substitute) and fish biosolids (an organic N source) — can match conventional inputs. 48 plants, 96 stems, six growth metrics recorded weekly across weeks 14–19, analyzed by one-way ANOVA + Tukey HSD (α = 0.05).

The paradox: 100% hemp produced significantly fewer leaves (p = 0.013) yet equal total leaf area (p = 0.235) via compensatory leaf expansion. Stem length, diameter, and truss count were statistically equivalent to perlite. The 50:50 blend was indistinguishable from both extremes.

The ceiling effect: leaf area rose in a highly significant dose-dependent manner, but 1X (400 mg N/L) was statistically equal to 4X (p = 0.134) — while 4X showed the lowest truss count, a signature of luxury N consumption. Stem length and diameter were unaffected across the whole 4× range.

Recommendation

A 50:50 hemp : perlite substrate paired with the minimum-effective 1X fish-biosolids rate is the most agronomically balanced, circular-economy formulation — matching conventional inputs while valorizing two waste streams.
